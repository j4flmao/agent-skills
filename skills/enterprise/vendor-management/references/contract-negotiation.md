# Contract Negotiation Guide

## Introduction

Contract negotiation establishes the legal and commercial framework for the vendor relationship. Effective negotiation balances cost with risk, ensures clear service expectations, and protects the organization's interests through well-defined terms.

## Master Services Agreement (MSA)

### MSA Structure
| Section | Content | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Recitals | Background and intent | Establishes context |
| Definitions | Key terms and meanings | Ensures unambiguous interpretation |
| Term and Termination | Duration, notice periods, termination rights | Governs contract lifecycle |
| Services | Scope description (detailed in SOWs) | Defines what vendor provides |
| Payment Terms | Fees, invoicing, payment schedule | Governs financial arrangements |
| Warranties | Service warranties and disclaimers | Sets service quality guarantees |
| Liability | Limitation of liability, exclusions | Caps financial exposure |
| IP Rights | Ownership of deliverables, pre-existing IP | Protects intellectual property |
| Confidentiality | Non-disclosure obligations | Protects sensitive information |
| Data Protection | Data handling, security, compliance | Meets regulatory requirements |
| Insurance | Required coverage types and limits | Ensures vendor carries adequate insurance |
| Dispute Resolution | Escalation, mediation, arbitration | Defines conflict resolution path |

## Statement of Work (SOW)

### SOW Components
- **Scope of Work**: Detailed description of deliverables and services
- **Acceptance Criteria**: How deliverables are evaluated and accepted
- **Timeline and Milestones**: Key dates, dependencies, and deliverables schedule
- **Pricing and Payment Schedule**: Fixed price, T&M, or milestone-based
- **Responsibilities**: Vendor and customer obligations
- **Assumptions**: Conditions and exclusions
- **Change Control**: How scope changes are managed
- **Sign-Off Process**: Formal acceptance and approval process

### SOW Change Control
- Change request form with impact assessment
- Pricing impact for scope changes
- Timeline impact assessment
- Approval authority based on change value
- Documentation and version control

## Key Contract Terms

### Service Level Agreement (SLA)
| Term | Description | Recommended Position |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| Availability | Service uptime percentage | 99.9% minimum (industry standard) |
| Performance | Response time, throughput | Clearly defined metrics with measurement method |
| Support Hours | Support availability and coverage | 24/7 for critical, business hours for standard |
| Incident Response | Time to respond per priority | P1: <=15 min, P2: <=30 min, P3: <=2 hours, P4: <=8 hours |
| Incident Resolution | Time to resolve per priority | P1: <=4 hours, P2: <=8 hours, P3: <=48 hours, P4: <=5 days |
| Service Credits | Penalty for missed SLA | 5-25% of monthly fee based on severity |
| SLA Reporting | Measurement and reporting frequency | Monthly detailed, real-time dashboard |

### Pricing Models
| Model | Description | Best For | Risk |
|-------|-------------|----------|------|
| Fixed Price | Single price for defined scope | Well-defined requirements | Overrun risk on vendor |
| Time and Materials | Hourly/daily rates for work | Evolving requirements | Budget uncertainty for customer |
| Subscription | Recurring fee per period | Ongoing services | Long-term commitment |
| Consumption-Based | Pay per use (transactions, storage) | Variable usage | Volume forecasting risk |
| Outcome-Based | Pay for achieved results | Performance-driven engagements | Measurement complexity |

### Termination Rights
| Termination Type | Notice Period | Payment Obligation |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| For Cause (Vendor breach) | Immediate | No further payment, refund for unused |
| For Cause (Customer breach) | 30 days to cure, then immediate | Payment for services rendered |
| For Convenience | 30-90 days | Payment for services rendered to termination date |
| For Insolvency | Immediate | No further payment |
| For Change of Control | 30 days from event | Mutual termination rights |

### Liability Terms
- **Liability Cap**: Typically 12 months' fees (negotiate from 6-24 months)
- **Exclusions from Cap**: IP infringement, breach of confidentiality, data breach, gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud
- **Indemnification**: Vendor indemnifies for IP infringement, data breach, regulatory penalties
- **Mutual vs. One-Sided**: Prefer mutual liability with vendor exceptions
- **Consequential Damages**: Mutual exclusion is standard practice

### Intellectual Property
| IP Type | Ownership | Terms |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Pre-existing IP | Vendor retains | License to customer for service use |
| Developed IP | Customer owns (preferred) | Work-for-hire or assignment clause |
| Improvements | Customer owns improvements to deliverables | Vendor may retain general improvements |
| Open Source | Must be disclosed | Compliance with applicable licenses |

### Data Protection Terms
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) as exhibit
- Data classification and handling requirements
- Data residency and sovereignty requirements
- Breach notification within 24-72 hours (per regulation)
- Right to audit vendor security practices
- Data return and deletion at termination
- Sub-processor approval rights

## Standard Clauses

### Most Favored Customer (MFC)
Ensures customer receives vendor's best pricing and terms.
- Request for strategic engagements and enterprise customers
- Typically restricted to pricing, not all terms
- Define scope clearly to avoid disputes

### Non-Solicitation
Restricts hiring of each other's employees during and after contract term.
- Typical duration: 12 months post-termination
- Exceptions for general public job postings
- Consider mutual or one-sided based on leverage

### Insurance Requirements
| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---------------|---------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $1-2 million per occurrence |
| Professional Liability / E&O | $1-5 million |
| Cyber Liability | $1-10 million depending on data access |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory |
| Umbrella/Excess Liability | $5-10 million |

### Audit Rights
- Right to audit vendor's security, financial, and operational controls
- Notice period: 10-30 business days
- Frequency: Annually (more for critical vendors)
- Cost: Customer bears cost unless material issues found
- Scope: Security controls, SLA compliance, data handling

## Negotiation Strategy

### Preparation
1. Define walk-away terms (must-have, want, nice-to-have)
2. Understand vendor's priorities (revenue, referenceability, growth)
3. Establish negotiation team and authority levels
4. Prepare competitive benchmarks from RFP process
5. Identify regulatory and compliance requirements

### Leverage Points
- Competitive alternatives (from RFP)
- Contract value and term commitment
- Strategic relationship potential
- Market timing (end of quarter, fiscal year)
- Regulatory compliance needs
- Integration dependency

### Common Pitfalls
- Over-focusing on price, under-focusing on terms
- Vague SLA definitions left for later
- No exit strategy or data retrieval plan
- Unlimited liability exposure
- Missing change control mechanism
- Unclear acceptance criteria for deliverables
