# Launch Tiers

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Major Launch
```
Scope: New product, major platform launch, new market entry
Duration: 8-12 weeks planning + 4-6 weeks execution
Resources: Dedicated GTM team, cross-functional, marketing budget

Channels:
  — Press/PR: Press release, media outreach, analyst briefings
  — Content: Blog post (long-form), case study, white paper
  — Product: In-app announcements, email blast to all users
  — Sales: Team enablement, collateral, demo video
  — Events: Webinar, conference presence (if applicable)
  — Paid: Targeted ad campaign, retargeting

Success Metrics:
  Signups: +X% above baseline
  Traffic: X visits in first month
  Revenue: X new MRR from launch
  Press coverage: X articles/mentions
```

### Tier 2 — Feature Launch
```
Scope: Significant new feature, major improvement
Duration: 4-6 weeks planning + 2-3 weeks execution
Resources: Product team + marketing support

Channels:
  — Content: Blog post (medium-length), feature highlight
  — Product: In-app announcement, targeted email
  — Sales: Briefing document, updated demo
  — Community: Forum post, social media

Success Metrics:
  Feature adoption: X% of users within 30 days
  Satisfaction: NPS > X for new feature
  Activation lift: +X% activation rate
```

### Tier 3 — Minor Release
```
Scope: Small feature, UX improvement, bug fix
Duration: 1-2 weeks planning + 1 week execution
Resources: Product team only

Channels:
  — Product: In-app notification, feature tooltip
  — Changelog: Update posted
  — Social: Single social post (optional)

Success Metrics:
  Feature adoption: X% of users within 30 days
  Support ticket reduction (if bug fix)
```

## Launch Playbook

### Pre-Launch (2-4 weeks before)
```
Internal:
  — Stakeholder alignment on messaging and goals
  — FAQ document for support and sales
  — Beta feedback incorporated into final product
  — Legal review (if applicable)

Product:
  — Feature complete and tested
  — Analytics tracking instrumented
  — Landing page ready (for T1/T2)
  — Documentation updated

Marketing (T1/T2):
  — Blog post drafted and reviewed
  — Email copy written and segmented
  — Social media posts scheduled
  — Press list prepared (T1)
```

### Launch Day
```
Execute:
  — Deploy feature (timed for business hours)
  — Monitor for errors and performance
  — Activate in-app announcements
  — Send email campaign (T1/T2)
  — Publish blog post (T1/T2)
  — Post on social channels

Monitor:
  — Error rates and performance
  — Feature adoption metrics
  — Support ticket volume
  — Social mentions
```

### Post-Launch (Week 1-4)
```
Week 1:
  — Monitor adoption and fix issues
  — Respond to feedback
  — Share early results internally

Week 2-4:
  — Second email to non-openers
  — Case study or results blog post (T1)
  -– Iterate based on feedback

Month 3:
  — Post-launch review with team
  — Document learnings
  — Plan iterations
```
