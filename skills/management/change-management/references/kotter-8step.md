# Kotter 8-Step Change Model

## Overview
John Kotter's 8-Step model is a framework for leading organizational transformation. Steps 1-4 create the climate for change, steps 5-7 engage and enable the organization, step 8 implements and sustains the change.

## Step 1: Create Urgency

### Goal
Help people see the need for change and the importance of acting immediately.

### Actions
Identify potential crises, opportunities, and competitive threats. Share compelling evidence (data, customer feedback, market trends). Paint a vivid picture of what's at stake. Remove complacency sources (excessive resources, low performance standards). Make the gap between current state and future state undeniable.

### Output
Urgency score: what percentage of the organization believes change is critical now? Target: >75% urgency level before proceeding.

### KPIs
Urgency survey score. Number of managers actively creating urgency. Speed of adoption of initial change messages. Management requests for more information.

### Failures
Over 50% of transformations fail at step 1. Underestimating complacency. Not enough evidence. Leadership sends mixed signals. Fear-based urgency creates resistance instead of motivation.

## Step 2: Build Guiding Coalition

### Goal
Assemble a group with enough power, credibility, and expertise to lead the change.

### Actions
Recruit key leaders from across the organization. Ensure coalition has position power, expertise, credibility, and leadership. Include at least one C-suite sponsor. Include skeptics (they strengthen the coalition when converted). Meet regularly as a coalition.

### Coalition Composition
Position power: enough senior leaders to block opposition. Expertise: deep knowledge of the change area. Credibility: respected and trusted by the organization. Leadership: can drive and inspire, not just manage.

### KPIs
Coalition formed and meets weekly. Coalition represents all key stakeholder groups. Coalition members publicly advocate for change. Coalition has decision-making authority.

## Step 3: Form Strategic Vision

### Goal
Create a clear, compelling, and achievable vision of the future state.

### Actions
Define the core change vision in 5 minutes or less. Connect vision to customer value and organizational purpose. Identify the strategy to achieve the vision. Test vision with diverse groups for clarity and emotional appeal. Make it simple enough to communicate in one sentence.

### Vision Test
Can you explain it in 5 minutes and get understanding? Does it appeal to both head (logic) and heart (emotion)? Is it desirable, feasible, and focused? Does it guide decision-making at all levels?

### KPIs
Vision understood and remembered by employees. Vision guides day-to-day decisions. Alignment between vision and actions of coalition. Employee ability to articulate vision.

## Step 4: Enlist Volunteer Army

### Goal
Communicate the vision widely and get buy-in across the organization.

### Actions
Communicate vision in every channel, repeatedly. Lead by example — coalition must model desired behaviors. Use simple, vivid language not jargon. Address concerns and questions directly. Connect vision to each group's daily work. Create two-way dialogue, not broadcast.

### Communication Cadence
Weekly all-hands during critical periods. Monthly departmental deep-dives. Quarterly progress reviews. Daily standups for teams directly involved. Communication is never one and done — it takes 10x repetition for a message to stick.

### KPIs
Vision understanding (surveys, Q&A). Willing volunteers stepping forward. Change-related initiatives proposed by non-coalition members. Communication reach and frequency metrics.

## Step 5: Enable Action by Removing Barriers

### Goal
Remove obstacles that block the vision — structural, systemic, and human.

### Actions
Identify barriers: processes, systems, policies, people, culture. Change systems that conflict with the vision. Address managers who undercut the change. Provide training and resources. Empower people to experiment and innovate. Remove unnecessary rules and approvals.

### Barrier Types
Structural: org chart, reporting lines, team boundaries. Systemic: performance metrics, compensation, budgeting, IT systems. Human: skills gaps, resistant managers, insufficient capacity. Cultural: norms, values, unwritten rules that conflict.

### KPIs
Number of barriers identified and removed. Time to resolve reported barriers. Employee perception of empowerment (survey). Speed of decision making for change-related initiatives.

## Step 6: Generate Short-Term Wins

### Goal
Create visible, unambiguous successes within the first 6-12 months.

### Actions
Identify early wins achievable with minimal resources. Plan wins visibly and publicize them. Celebrate the people who made wins happen. Use wins to build credibility and momentum. Analyze what made wins work and replicate pattern.

### Win Characteristics
Visible: everyone can see them. Unambiguous: clearly a result of the change. Connected to the vision: directly demonstrate progress. Achievable within 6 months of start. Meaningful: matter to key stakeholders.

### KPIs
Number and visibility of short-term wins. Win impact on key change metrics. Change in employee belief that change is working. Coalition credibility scores.

## Step 7: Sustain Acceleration

### Goal
Use credibility from early wins to tackle bigger changes and build momentum.

### Actions
After first wins, increase scope and ambition. Bring more people into the change effort. Interlock change initiatives for compounding impact. Remove additional layers of barriers. Reinvigorate the coalition if energy is flagging. Keep urgency high — don't declare victory too soon.

### Acceleration Indicators
More volunteers step forward. Change initiatives spawn sub-initiatives. People surface and remove barriers without being asked. Change is becoming self-sustaining.

### KPIs
Velocity of change adoption. Number of active change initiatives. Employee-initiated improvements. Depth of change (surface to structural). Speed of subsequent wins.

## Step 8: Institute Change

### Goal
Anchor changes in corporate culture, systems, and practices.

### Actions
Connect new behaviors to organizational success. Build change into succession planning and hiring criteria. Update performance systems, compensation, and promotions. Create stories and narratives around the change journey. Hand off ownership from coalition to operating managers.

### Institutionalization
New behaviors become the norm. Old ways are forgotten or actively rejected. Change is embedded in hiring, onboarding, training. Metrics continue to improve without active change management. Successors are committed to maintaining the change.

### KPIs
Change persistence after coalition disbands. New hire adoption of changed practices. Manager commitment to maintaining change. Regression rate (return to old ways). Long-term outcome metrics (12-24 months).

## Key Points
Steps must be executed in order — skipping steps causes failure.
Urgency must be >75% before proceeding to step 2.
Short-term wins must be visible within first 6 months.
Half of all transformations fail at step 1 — invest here.
Change sticks when it's embedded in culture, not just process.
The coalition is the engine of change — invest in their energy.
Never declare victory before the change is institutionalized.
