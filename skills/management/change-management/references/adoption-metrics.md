# Adoption Metrics

## Overview
Adoption metrics measure how successfully a change is being adopted by the target audience. They provide objective data on progress, identify gaps, and guide course-correction. Without measurement, change management is guesswork.

## Adoption Rate

### Definition
The percentage of the target population that has adopted the new process, tool, or behavior within a specified time period. Adoption = number of active adopters / total target population × 100.

### Measurement Methods
System-based: usage logs, login frequency, feature utilization. Observable: managers report team adoption status. Survey-based: self-reported adoption level. Process-based: completion of required training or certification.

### Benchmarks
Month 1: 20-30% adoption (early adopters, innovators). Month 3: 50-60% adoption (early majority joins). Month 6: 70-80% adoption (late majority joins). Month 12: >90% adoption (laggards adopt, stragglers identified). Adjust benchmarks by change complexity and organizational readiness.

### Tracking
Measure weekly in first month, monthly through month 6, quarterly thereafter. Segment by department, role, region, demographic. Compare adoption curves across groups. Flag groups below 50% of organizational average.

## Time-to-Adoption

### Definition
The average time between change launch and when a user demonstrates consistent, independent adoption. Time-to-adoption = date of first sustained use minus change launch date.

### Measurement
Define "sustained use" for each change: completed training + performed key action 3 times independently, or logged in and used core feature for 5 consecutive days, or completed first batch of work using new process. Track per user. Report median, p25, p75 to understand distribution.

### Segmentation
Compare TTA by role, department, tenure, tech proficiency. Identify which segments adopt fastest and slowest. Investigate slow segments for barriers or support gaps. Use TTA variance as a signal — high variance means inconsistent enablement.

### Improvement
Reduce friction: simpler process, better tools, fewer steps. Improve support: coaching, peer buddies, help desk responsiveness. Address specific barriers per slow segment. Adjust training timing and format based on feedback.

## Feature Usage

### Depth of Adoption
Not all uses are equal — measure depth, not just binary adoption.
Logged in (shallow): user has accessed the tool.
Tried core feature (basic): user has attempted the primary function.
Regular user (consistent): user returns regularly to perform key tasks.
Power user (advanced): user leverages advanced features, shortcuts, integrations.
Champion (advocate): user actively promotes and teaches others.

### Feature-Specific Metrics
For each new feature or process, track: adoption rate (percentage using it), frequency of use (daily, weekly, monthly), feature stickiness (return rate after first use), feature drop-off (used once then abandoned). Identify features with high drop-off for root cause analysis.

### Usage Quality
Not just whether they use it, but how well. Error rate: how often do users make mistakes? Reversion rate: how often do users fall back to old ways? Assistance rate: how often do they need help with the new way? Completion rate: do they complete the new process or abandon mid-way?

## Proficiency Assessment

### Proficiency Levels
Unaware: doesn't know the change exists. Aware: knows about it but hasn't tried. Novice: can perform basic tasks with support. Competent: performs regular tasks independently. Proficient: handles exceptions and edge cases. Expert: teaches others, improves the process.

### Assessment Methods
Observation: manager or coach observes and rates performance. Self-assessment: user rates their own proficiency against defined criteria. Test: knowledge assessment or practical demonstration. System data: behavior patterns indicate proficiency (speed, error rate, feature use). Peer assessment: colleagues rate each other's proficiency.

### Proficiency Goals
Month 1: reach Novice level (basic tasks with support). Month 3: reach Competent level (independent). Month 6: Proficient level (handles exceptions). Month 12: Expert level (teaches others, improvement). Track percentage of users at each level over time.

## Net Promoter Score (NPS)

### Definition
NPS measures how likely users are to recommend the product to others. Based on single question: "How likely are you to recommend [product/change] to a colleague?" Scale 0-10. Promoters (9-10), Passives (7-8), Detractors (0-6). NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors. Range: -100 to +100.

### Contextual NPS
For change management, NPS at different stages: launch NPS (first impression), 30-day NPS (initial experience), 90-day NPS (settled experience), annual NPS (long-term satisfaction). Compare scores across stakeholder groups. Investigate low-score groups for specific issues.

### Using NPS
NPS above 30 indicates healthy adoption. Above 50 indicates strong advocacy. Below 0 indicates serious problems. Segment NPS by adoption level: non-adopters likely detractors, power users likely promoters. NPS trends matter more than absolute scores.

## Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS)

### Definition
eNPS measures employee loyalty and satisfaction with the organization. Question: "How likely are you to recommend this company as a place to work?" Same 0-10 scale and calculation as NPS.

### Change-Specific eNPS
During change, measure: change eNPS: "How likely are you to recommend this change to others?" Support eNPS: "How likely are you to recommend the change support (training, resources) to a colleague?" Leadership eNPS: "How confident are you in leadership's ability to manage this change?" Compare change eNPS to organizational eNPS for context.

## Measurement Framework Design

### Define Metrics
For each change: primary metric (overall adoption rate), secondary metrics (TTA, proficiency, NPS), segment metrics (by group, role, region), leading indicators (training completion, early logins, support tickets), lagging indicators (retention, performance, satisfaction).

### Set Targets
Adoption rate: X% by month 3, Y% by month 6. Time-to-adoption: target median TTA in days. Proficiency: X% at Competent level by month 3. NPS: target score by month 3 and month 6. Thresholds: minimum acceptable, target, stretch.

### Collection and Reporting
Automate system metrics where possible (usage logs, feature tracking). Schedule survey-based metrics at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly). Create dashboard with trend lines and segment views. Report to stakeholders at each change phase. Adjust targets and interventions based on data.

## Key Points
Adoption rate without proficiency measurement hides shallow adoption.
Time-to-adoption reveals where the process or support is failing.
Feature depth (not just binary use) shows true adoption quality.
NPS and eNPS provide sentiment context for behavioral metrics.
Segment metrics by stakeholder group — organization-wide averages hide problems.
Leading indicators predict outcomes before lagging metrics reveal them.
Measure continuously, not just once — adoption curves reveal progress and risks.
