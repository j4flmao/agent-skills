# Interview Process Design

## Overview
A structured interview pipeline ensures consistent, bias-resistant
candidate evaluation from application to offer. Each stage has a
clear purpose, defined format, and decision gating. The process
is documented, repeatable, and measurable.

## Pipeline Stages

### Stage 1: Application and Screening
Resume reviewed against 5-7 must-have criteria.
Criteria defined before job posting — never added during review.
Auto-reject if missing critical requirements.

Phone screen (30 min): recruiter or hiring manager.
Cover: motivation, availability, salary expectations,
basic role alignment (can they talk about their experience?).

Score screen 1-5: 1-2 reject, 3 advance, 4-5 strong advance.
Go/no-go gate clearly documented.

### Stage 2: Technical Assessment
Choose ONE format per role:

Take-home: practical real-world task.
4-8 hours, compensated for senior roles.
Higher dropout rate (>50%) but deeper signal.

Async coding: HackerRank or CodeSignal.
Algorithmic or domain-specific, 30-90 min.
Good for high-volume screening.

Pairing session: 45 min live coding with interviewer.
Preferred format — best signal on collaboration and communication.

For senior roles: system design deep-dive or architecture review.
Decision gate: technical score determines onsite advancement.

### Stage 3: Onsite
Typically 3-4 sessions with breaks between.
Each session scored independently — no shared rubrics mid-process.
Prevents anchoring and groupthink.

Coding (45-60 min): algorithm or domain problem.
Scored on 4 rubric dimensions.

System Design (45-60 min): architecture problem.
Scored on 4 rubric dimensions.

Behavioral (45 min): STAR questions aligned to company values.
Scored on 4 rubric dimensions.

Optional: debugging session, API design, or team presentation.
Interviewers submit scores within 1 hour of session end.

### Stage 4: Debrief
Schedule within 24 hours of onsite completion.
Attendees: all interviewers, hiring manager, recruiter.

Format:
Each submits written scores before meeting.
Meeting: each shares score and rationale.
Interviewer who observed candidate goes first.

Discrepancy resolution:
Discuss if they observed different signals
or interpreted same signal differently.
Focus on behaviors, not personalities.

Rules:
No ranking or comparison to other candidates.
Evaluate against rubric only.

Decisions:
- Hire: average >= 3, no dimension < 2
- No-hire: average < 2.5, any critical < 2
- Leaning-no: discuss, consider extra interview

### Stage 5: Offer
Compile offer:
Base salary range (determined before interviews).
Equity/stocks, signing bonus, start date, onboarding plan.

Reference checks: 2-3 professional references.
Ask about: collaboration, growth, impact, areas for development.
Verify must-haves against reference feedback.

Offer expiration: 7-10 business days standard.
Counteroffer strategy: know max before negotiation.

## Candidate Experience

### Pre-Onsite
Send schedule and interviewer names 24 hours before.
Clear instructions: what to prepare, tools, format, breaks.
Ask about accommodation needs proactively.

### Post-Onsite
Debrief feedback within 48 hours.
Rejection: within 24 hours with specific, actionable feedback.
No ghosting — every candidate receives outcome.

Collect NPS: "How likely to recommend this process?"
Score tracks process quality over time.

### Metrics
Time-to-fill: target 30-45 days.
Stage completion rate: track drop-off per stage.
Offer acceptance rate: target >80%.
Candidate NPS: target >50.
Demographic breakdown at each stage.

## Bias Mitigation

### Structured Process
Same questions, same rubric, same time, same criteria.
For every candidate for the same role.
Questions selected before interview.
No mid-interview question changes.

### Panel Diversity
At least one interviewer from underrepresented group.
Rotate interviewers across candidates.
Prevents panel bias from affecting specific candidates.

### Score First, Discuss Second
Prevents anchoring (first opinion shared).
Prevents groupthink (majority influencing others).

### Blind Resume Review
Remove name, photo, university, graduation year.
Review against must-have criteria only.

### Decision Criteria
Defined before interviews begin — no moving goalposts.
If criteria change mid-process, restart for all in-flight candidates.

## Key Points
Structured interviews predict performance 2-3x better than unstructured.
Every interviewer must complete training before conducting.
Covers: bias awareness, structured technique, rubric calibration.
Collect NPS after every onsite — track trend.
Document every decision with specific evidence, not general impressions.
Candidate drop-off highest at take-home stage — consider pairing instead.
