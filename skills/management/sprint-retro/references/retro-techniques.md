# Retro Techniques

## Start/Stop/Continue

### Structure
| Start (new practices) | Stop (harmful practices) | Continue (good practices) |
|------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Pair programming for complex tickets | Merging without code review | Daily standups |
| Automated E2E tests on every PR | Deploying on Friday afternoon | Sprint demos |

### Best For
- Balanced, normal sprints (no dominant emotional event)
- New teams establishing their working agreements
- Teams that have not done a retro before (lowest barrier to entry)
- Time-constrained retros (30 min or less)

### Facilitation Tips
- First round: everyone writes at least 1 item per column
- Second round: add more if ideas remain
- Group related items in each column
- Focus on Stop items first — removing bad practices is easier than adding new ones
- A Continue column vote of 80%+ on an item means "this is working, protect it"

## 4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For)

### Structure
| Liked (positive) | Learned (insights) | Lacked (missing) | Longed For (desires) |
|------------------|-------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| Smooth deployment pipeline | How to debug production issues | Better test documentation | More cross-team collaboration |

### Best For
- Deep reflection after a significant milestone (launch, migration, release)
- Experienced teams that need to go beyond surface-level discussion
- Teams that have used Start/Stop/Continue for the last 2-3 retros

### Facilitation Tips
- Start with Liked to put the team in a positive frame of mind
- Longed For tends to produce deeper, more constructive items than Lacked
- If Lacked is empty, the team may be avoiding conflict — probe gently
- Learned items can be turned into knowledge-sharing sessions or tech talks
- Longed For items often need more discussion to turn into action items

## Sailboat (Wind / Anchor / Rocks / Iceberg)

### Structure
| Wind (helps us move) | Anchor (slows us down) | Rocks (known risks) | Iceberg (hidden dangers) |
|----------------------|------------------------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Strong team collaboration | Heavy approval processes | Losing a team member | New competitor entering market |

### Best For
- Complex projects with multiple factors affecting progress
- Teams that respond well to metaphor and visualization
- Surfacing hidden risks and systemic issues
- Retros where the team is struggling to articulate what is wrong

### Facilitation Tips
- Draw the sailboat on a whiteboard or digital canvas
- Wind items go in the sails (above the boat), Anchor items below the boat
- Rocks are visible risks (above water), Icebergs are hidden (below water)
- Iceberg items often reveal the most important insights
- Follow up: how do we increase wind, lift anchors, avoid rocks, and watch for icebergs?

## Mad / Sad / Glad

### Structure
| Mad (frustrations) | Sad (disappointments) | Glad (positive moments) |
|--------------------|-----------------------|-------------------------|
| Unplanned work derailed sprint | Missed the release deadline | Shipped the new search feature |

### Best For
- Teams recovering from a tough sprint or incident
- Building psychological safety and emotional awareness
- New teams where trust is still developing
- Teams with low energy or morale

### Facilitation Tips
- Normalize all emotions — there are no wrong feelings
- Start with Glad to ensure the retro isn't entirely negative
- Watch for personal attacks in Mad items — redirect to processes
- Sad items often reveal unmet expectations — discuss how to set better expectations
- If Mad column is overwhelmingly large, consider a dedicated problem-solving session outside the retro

## Timeline / Time-Line Retro

### Structure
Draw a horizontal timeline of the sprint. Team members place significant events (positive and negative) along the timeline. Discuss patterns and turning points.

### Best For
- Longer retrospectives (60-90 min)
- Multi-week sprints or release retrospectives
- Teams that need to understand sequence and causality
- Post-incident or post-launch reviews

### Facilitation Tips
- Create the timeline before the retro (add sprint events, releases, incidents)
- Each person adds their own significant moments
- Look for clusters — several people noting the same event signals importance
- Discuss the emotional arc of the sprint — when was energy high, when was it low?
- Identify turning points — events that changed the trajectory of the sprint

## DACI (Drivers, Approvers, Contributors, Informed)

### Structure
A decision-focused retrospective format. For each major decision in the sprint, identify:
- Who was the Driver?
- Who were the Approvers?
- Who were the Contributors?
- Who should have been Informed?

### Best For
- Teams that experienced decision-making issues
- Post-mortems on projects with unclear ownership
- Analyzing communication breakdowns

## Circle of Influence

### Structure
Draw three concentric circles:
1. **Control** (inner): Things we can directly change
2. **Influence** (middle): Things we can influence but not control
3. **Concern** (outer): Things we care about but cannot control or influence

Sort retro items into the three circles. Action items must come from the Control circle.

### Best For
- Teams feeling overwhelmed by external factors
- Retros where many items are outside the team's control
- Shifting from complaining to action

## Speed Boat (Product Version)

### Structure
Imagine the product as a speedboat. Anchors are what slow it down (bugs, usability issues, missing features). Engines are what make it go fast (great features, performance, UX).

### Best For
- Product-focused teams doing a product-level retro
- Combining process improvement with product feedback

## Format Selection Guide

| Mood | Sprint Outcome | Recommended Format |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Neutral | Balanced | Start/Stop/Continue |
| Reflective | Post-milestone | 4Ls |
| Complex | Many factors | Sailboat |
| Emotional | Tough sprint | Mad/Sad/Glad |
| Overwhelmed | External pressures | Circle of Influence |
| Low engagement | Repetitive issues | Timeline |
| Confused about decisions | Poor decisions | DACI |
