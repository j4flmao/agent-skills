# Brand Identity Advanced Topics

## Overview
Advanced brand identity extends beyond basics to multi-brand systems, brand architecture (house of brands vs branded house), global/local adaptation, co-branding, rebranding strategy, and measuring brand equity.

## Advanced Concepts

### Concept 1: Brand Architecture
House of brands (Procter & Gamble: Tide, Pampers) vs branded house (Google: Gmail, Maps, Drive) vs endorsed brands (Marriott: Courtyard, Ritz-Carlton). Each model has different visual identity implications — shared elements vs distinct identities.

### Concept 2: Global vs Local
Global brands must adapt to local markets without losing recognition. Strategies: uniform (same everywhere), adapted (same core, local execution), or local (brand name known, rest is local). Consider: language (alphabet length varying), color meanings (white = purity in West, mourning in East), symbolism.

### Concept 3: Rebranding Strategy
Rebranding continuum: refresh (evolutionary, minimal change) → reposition (new audience, same brand) → full rebrand (new name, new identity). Each requires different rollout strategy: phased (features first, logo later) or big bang (everything at once).

### Concept 4: Co-Branding
Co-branding requires clear rules: spacing between logos, sizing parity, color treatment, placement hierarchy. Document co-branding templates for common scenarios (partnership, sponsorship, integration).

### Concept 5: Brand Equity Measurement
Quantitative: awareness (recall/recognition), perception (attribute ratings), loyalty (NPS, repurchase), financial (brand valuation, price premium). Qualitative: brand meaning, emotional connection, cultural relevance. Track trends over time.

## Advanced Techniques

### Brand Audit Process
1. Inventory all touchpoints (digital, print, physical, environmental)
2. Score each against guidelines (0=violation, 1=partial, 2=full)
3. Calculate brand consistency score
4. Identify top 5 inconsistencies
5. Create remediation plan with owners and timeline
6. Track score quarterly

### Design Token Implementation
Convert brand values to code-level tokens: `--brand-primary: #0052CC`, `--brand-secondary: #00B8D9`, `--brand-typeface: 'Inter', sans-serif`. Distribute via npm package, CocoaPods, or Maven. Tokens are consumed by design system component libraries.

### Rebranding Rollout Plan
| Phase | Activities | Timeline |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Research | Audit, competitive analysis, stakeholder interviews | 4-6 weeks |
| Strategy | Positioning, messaging, naming (if needed) | 2-4 weeks |
| Visual | Logo, color, typography, imagery exploration | 4-8 weeks |
| Refine | Testing, iteration, finalize | 2-4 weeks |
| Guidelines | Documentation, templates, toolkit | 2-4 weeks |
| Launch | Internal launch → external launch → migration | 4-12 weeks |
| Monitor | Audit, track adoption, iterate | Ongoing |

## Anti-Patterns

- Rebranding without strategic rationale (aesthetic changes only)
- Killing brand equity by changing everything at once
- Ignoring local market needs in global brand execution
- Overly prescriptive guidelines that don't fit real-world applications
- Neglecting brand governance after launch
- Co-branding without clear rules (logo placement disputes)
- Brand identity designed by committee (diluted results)
