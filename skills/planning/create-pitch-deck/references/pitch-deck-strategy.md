# Pitch Deck Strategy

## Narrative Structure

### The Story Arc
```
1. Hook (Slide 1) — Grab attention with one clear statement
2. Problem (Slides 2-3) — Define the pain point
3. Solution (Slides 4-5) — Your product/service
4. Market (Slide 6) — TAM, SAM, SOM
5. Product (Slides 7-8) — Demo, screenshots, key features
6. Traction (Slides 9-10) — Metrics, customers, growth
7. Business Model (Slide 11) — How you make money
8. Competition (Slide 12) — Competitive landscape
9. Team (Slide 13) — Why you're the right team
10. Financials (Slide 14) — Projections, burn rate
11. Ask (Slide 15) — What you need and what it buys
```

## Slide Design Principles

### Visual Rules
- One idea per slide — no crowded slides
- Use visuals over text — charts, diagrams, screenshots
- Maximum 3 bullet points per slide
- Font size minimum 24pt for body text
- Consistent color palette and typography

### Data Presentation
- Use simple charts (bar, line, donut) — avoid complex visualizations
- Label axes clearly — never assume audience knows
- Highlight the key takeaway above each chart
- Include source attribution for market data

## Investor Psychology

### What Investors Look For
| Factor | Weight | Evidence |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Team | 40% | Founder-market fit, domain expertise |
| Market size | 25% | Large TAM with growth trajectory |
| Traction | 20% | Revenue, users, partnerships |
| Product | 10% | Differentiation, defensibility |
| Business model | 5% | Unit economics, margins |

### Common Questions to Prepare For
- Why now? (timing)
- Why you? (team)
- What's the moat? (defensibility)
- How will you spend the money? (use of funds)
- What's the exit strategy? (acquisition targets, IPO timeline)

## Pitching Tips

### Delivery
- Practice 30-second, 3-minute, and 10-minute versions
- Know your numbers without reading slides
- Anticipate objections and prepare responses
- Read the room — adjust pace based on engagement

### Follow-up
- Send deck within 24 hours
- Include one-pager executive summary
- Reference specific conversation points from the meeting
- Propose next steps with timeline
