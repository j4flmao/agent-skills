# Investor Questions

Common investor questions organized by pitch deck slide, with guidance
on how to answer effectively.

## Slide 1: Problem

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| How do you know this is a real problem? | Do you have primary research or data, or is it an assumption? |
| Who has this problem today? | Is this a broad market or a niche? |
| How do people solve this today? | Are existing solutions "good enough"? Is there switching inertia? |
| Why hasn't this been solved before? | Is there a structural barrier or were previous attempts flawed? |
| Is this a painkiller or a vitamin? | Will customers pay or just "like" it? |

**How to answer:** Lead with specific data. "We interviewed 100
engineers and 78% said they spend > 5 hours/week on this task."
Avoid abstract descriptions of the problem.

## Slide 2: Solution

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Why will your solution work when others failed? | What is your unique insight? |
| Can this be built? | Is there technical risk you are underestimating? |
| What is the core mechanism that makes it work? | Do you understand why your solution is effective? |
| How is this different from a feature? | Is this a company or a feature of an existing product? |
| What is the hardest part of building this? | Have you thought through the challenges? |

**How to answer:** Focus on the "why" behind your approach, not just
the "what." If there is technical innovation, explain it simply.

## Slide 3: Market Size

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| How did you calculate TAM? | Is this defensible or fantasy? |
| What is your SAM and why? | Can you actually reach this segment? |
| Why will this market grow? | Is there a secular trend or is it static? |
| Who are the incumbents and what is their revenue? | Is this a replacement market or new? |
| What is the bottom-up SOM calculation? | Do you understand the unit economics of customer acquisition? |

**How to answer:** Show both top-down and bottom-up calculations.
Cite specific sources for each number. Be conservative. "We believe
$100M is achievable, not $1B."

## Slide 4: Product

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| What is the magic moment? | When does the user realize value? How long does it take? |
| What is the user retention curve? | Do users come back after day 1, 7, 30? |
| What is the biggest user complaint? | Are you listening to feedback? |
| How does the user discover the key feature? | Is the UX intuitive or does it need onboarding? |
| What is the tech stack? | Are you using modern, scalable technology? |

**How to answer:** Show the product working. Demo video > screenshots
> descriptions. Include real user feedback, not projected.

## Slide 5: Traction

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| What is your week-over-week growth? | Is the trajectory accelerating or plateauing? |
| What is your cohort retention? | Do users who joined 3 months ago still use the product? |
| What channel is driving growth? | Do you have repeatable acquisition or one-time spikes? |
| How much did you spend on customer acquisition? | What is your CAC and how is it trending? |
| What is the unit economics? | Do you make money on each customer or lose money? |

**How to answer:** Show the growth curve, not just the latest number.
Cohort retention tables are more important than total registered
users. Be honest about what is not working.

## Slide 6: Business Model

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Why did you choose this pricing model? | Have you tested price sensitivity? |
| What is your gross margin? | Is this a high-margin software business? |
| What is your CAC payback period? | How long until a customer becomes profitable? |
| What is your LTV/CAC ratio? | Is the unit economy sustainable? (>3x is target) |
| How does pricing change as customers scale? | Will enterprise customers pay more or less per unit? |

**How to answer:** Unit economics must be clear. "CAC is $200, LTV
is $800, payback is 4 months, gross margin is 82%." If some numbers
are unknown for early stage, state the assumption.

## Slide 7: Team

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Why is this team the right one? | Do you have domain expertise or startup experience? |
| What are the gaps on the team? | Are you self-aware about missing skills? |
| Who would you hire next? | Do you have a hiring plan? |
| Have you worked together before? | Does the team have trust and chemistry? |
| Why do you care about this problem? | Is this a mission or a quick flip? |

**How to answer:** Highlight specific relevant experience. "I built
the payments system at Stripe that processes $10B/year." Acknowledge
gaps and have a plan to fill them.

## Slide 8: Competition

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Who is your most dangerous competitor? | Are you ignoring a real threat? |
| What prevents Google/Meta/Amazon from building this? | Is there a defensible moat? |
| What is your competitive advantage? | Is it network effects, data, or switching costs? |
| How will you respond when a competitor copies your feature? | Do you have a strategy beyond "first mover"? |
| What does the competitive landscape look like in 3 years? | Are you thinking ahead? |

**How to answer:** Respect competitors. Never dismiss them.
Positioning map with clear axes is better than a feature matrix.
Highlight defensible moats, not just feature differences.

## Slide 9: Financials

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| What are the key assumptions in your model? | Have you identified the drivers, or is this a spreadsheet fantasy? |
| When do you reach breakeven? | Do you need infinite funding or is there a path? |
| What happens if growth is 50% of projection? | Have you considered downside scenarios? |
| How does the unit economics change at scale? | Does your CAC go up or down as you grow? |
| What is the burn rate and runway? | Are you capital efficient? |

**How to answer:** Present 3 scenarios (base, upside, downside).
Identify the 3 assumptions that most affect the model. Show that
you understand capital efficiency.

## Slide 10: Ask

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Is this amount justified by your plan? | Can you trace every dollar to a specific outcome? |
| What happens if you raise less? | What is the minimum viable raise? |
| What happens if you raise more? | Will you waste money? |
| What are the milestones for the next round? | Will this amount get you to a clear next fundraising event? |
| Are other investors committed? | Is there momentum or am I the first? |

**How to answer:** Be specific. "$2M to hire 4 engineers ($600K),
launch mobile app ($300K), acquire 10K users ($600K), and 18 months
runway ($500K)." Every dollar should map to a milestone.

## General Questions

| Question | What Investors Are Really Asking |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| What keeps you up at night? | Are you thoughtful about risks? |
| What would you do differently if starting over? | Have you learned from mistakes? |
| Why will you succeed? | Is there real conviction or just optimism? |
| How much of your own money is in this? | How committed are you? |
| What is the exit timeline? | Are you building for IPO or acquisition? |

## Psychology of Investor Questions

- **Pattern matching** — Investors compare your answers to hundreds of
  other pitches. Be different where it matters.
- **Conviction over data** — At early stages, conviction matters more
  than data. Show you will find a way.
- **Confidence without arrogance** — Acknowledge risks while showing
  you have a plan. Defensiveness is a red flag.
- **Brevity** — Answers should be 30-60 seconds. If it takes longer
  to explain, you have not simplified enough.
