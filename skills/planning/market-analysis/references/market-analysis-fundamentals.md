# Market Analysis Fundamentals

## What is Market Analysis?
Market analysis is the process of sizing a market opportunity, understanding the competitive landscape, and identifying strategic positioning. It answers four questions: how big is the opportunity, who else is chasing it, what are our advantages, and what trends will reshape the market.

## Core Components

### TAM/SAM/SOM
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): The total revenue opportunity if every potential customer bought your product.
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): The portion of TAM you can reach given your business model, geography, and customer profile.
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in 3-5 years.

Use two methods: top-down (start with analyst data, apply filters) and bottom-up (start with unit economics, multiply by customers). Reconcile the results.

### Competitor Analysis
Segment competitors into:
- **Direct**: Same problem, same solution category
- **Indirect**: Same problem, different solution
- **"Do nothing"**: The status quo

For each, analyze: features, pricing, target customers, market share, funding, strengths, weaknesses.

### SWOT Analysis
Internal: Strengths, Weaknesses. External: Opportunities, Threats. Every quadrant should have at least 3 items. Be honest about weaknesses — empty quadrants are not credible.

### Market Trends
Categorize into: technology shifts, regulatory changes, consumer behavior changes, competitive dynamics. Assess impact on your product.

### Positioning Map
A 2x2 matrix with relevant axes. Plot competitors. Your position should be in a clear, defensible sweet spot.

## Rules

**Source every number**: Unattributed market sizes are guesses, not data.

**Use both methods**: Top-down and bottom-up. Reconcile the difference.

**Features are not strategy**: The feature matrix reveals parity; SWOT reveals strategic differences.

**Be honest about weaknesses**: A SWOT with no weaknesses is not credible.

**Update quarterly**: Analysis older than 6 months is stale.

**Distinguish direct from indirect competitors**: Both matter but require different responses.

## Common Mistakes

**TAM fantasy**: Using the largest possible market definition without considering realistic segments. SAM should not be >50% of TAM.

**Confirmation bias**: Only selecting data that supports your conclusion. Every analysis should include a "risks to our thesis" section.

**Static analysis**: Treating the market as fixed. Markets change — competitors enter, merge, and exit.

**Ignoring indirect competitors**: Focusing only on direct competitors while adjacent solutions eat your market.

**No weaknesses**: A SWOT with all strengths is not credible. Every organization has weaknesses.
