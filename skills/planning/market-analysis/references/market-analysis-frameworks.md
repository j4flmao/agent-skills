# Market Analysis Frameworks

## TAM, SAM, SOM

### Definitions
| Term | Meaning | Example (Coffee Shop) |
|------|---------|-----------------------|
| TAM | Total Addressable Market | All coffee consumed globally |
| SAM | Serviceable Available Market | Coffee drinkers within delivery range |
| SOM | Serviceable Obtainable Market | Coffee drinkers you can realistically reach |

### Calculation Methods
```
Top-Down: Start with macro figure, narrow down
  Global market for X = $50B
  Addressable segment = 20% → $10B SAM
  Realistic capture = 5% → $500M SOM

Bottom-Up: Start with micro unit economics
  Price per unit = $10
  Target customers = 100,000
  Annual revenue = $10 × 100,000 = $1M SOM
```

## Competitive Analysis

### Porter's Five Forces
| Force | High | Low |
|-------|------|-----|
| Threat of new entrants | Easy to copy, low capital | Patented tech, high capital |
| Supplier power | Few suppliers, unique materials | Many suppliers, commoditized |
| Buyer power | Few large buyers | Many small buyers |
| Substitute threat | Easy alternatives available | Hard to replace |
| Competitive rivalry | Many similar competitors | Few differentiated players |

### Competitive Matrix
| Feature | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---------|----|-------------|-------------|
| Price | $$ | $ | $$$ |
| Speed | Fast | Slow | Medium |
| Integration | API + Webhook | Manual | API only |
| Support | 24/7 Chat | Email only | Chat (business hours) |

## Market Sizing Methods

### Surveys
- Ask target users directly about willingness to pay
- Validate with sample size > 300 for statistical significance
- Segment by persona for differentiated pricing

### Proxy Markets
- Use similar established market as growth proxy
- Apply relevant multipliers for differences
- Adjust for geography, segment, and timing

### Analogous Markets
- Study how similar products performed in adjacent markets
- Apply penetration rate from analogous market
- Adjust for market maturity differences
